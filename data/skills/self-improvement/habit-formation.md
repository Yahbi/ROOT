# Habit Formation

Build lasting habits using evidence-based behavior change strategies.

## Habit Loop (Cue → Routine → Reward)

### Components
- **Cue**: The trigger that initiates the behavior (time, location, preceding event, emotion)
- **Routine**: The behavior itself (the habit you want to build or break)
- **Reward**: The benefit you get from the behavior (satisfaction, progress, dopamine)

### Designing New Habits
1. **Make the cue obvious**: Place triggers in your environment (gym bag by door, book on pillow)
2. **Make the routine easy**: Start absurdly small (1 push-up, read 1 page, code for 5 minutes)
3. **Make the reward satisfying**: Track completion (checkbox), celebrate small wins
4. **Never miss twice**: Missing once is human; missing twice starts a new habit of not doing it

## Habit Stacking

### Formula
"After I [current habit], I will [new habit]."

### Examples
| Current Habit | New Habit | Stack |
|--------------|-----------|-------|
| After I pour morning coffee | I will review my goals for 2 minutes | Coffee → Goals |
| After I sit at my desk | I will write today's top 3 priorities | Desk → Priorities |
| After I close my laptop for the day | I will journal for 5 minutes | Close laptop → Journal |
| After I commit code | I will write one test for it | Commit → Test |

### Why It Works
- Existing habits are reliable cues (they already have strong neural pathways)
- Removes the decision of "when should I do this?" — it's automatic
- Chain multiple small habits into a routine (morning routine = 5+ stacked habits)

## Starting Small (The 2-Minute Rule)

### Principle
- Any new habit should take less than 2 minutes to start
- "Read before bed" becomes "read one page before bed"
- "Run every morning" becomes "put on running shoes every morning"
- The goal: become the type of person who does this, not to achieve a specific result yet

### Progression
1. Week 1-2: 2-minute version (just show up)
2. Week 3-4: 5-10 minute version (engage slightly more)
3. Month 2: 15-30 minute version (real practice)
4. Month 3+: Full version of the habit (identity established)

## Breaking Bad Habits

### Inversion Strategy
1. **Make the cue invisible**: Remove triggers (uninstall apps, avoid locations)
2. **Make the routine difficult**: Add friction (password manager logs out of social media)
3. **Make the reward unsatisfying**: Track the negative impact (money spent, time wasted)

### Substitution
- Don't just stop a bad habit — replace it with a better one for the same cue
- Stress eating → stress walking (same cue, different routine)
- Mindless scrolling → 5-minute reading (same downtime cue, productive routine)

## Tracking and Accountability

### Habit Tracking
- Visual tracking (calendar with X marks, app streaks) reinforces consistency
- Track daily for 30 days minimum — habits typically solidify in 21-66 days
- Weekly review: which habits stuck? Which need adjustment?

### Accountability Systems
| Method | Effectiveness | Effort |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Self-tracking | Medium | Low |
| Accountability partner | High | Medium |
| Public commitment | High | Medium |
| Stakes (bet money) | Very high | Low |

## Common Failure Modes

- Starting too many habits at once (max 1-2 new habits at a time)
- Making habits too ambitious from the start (leads to quitting)
- Relying on motivation instead of systems (motivation fluctuates, systems persist)
- Not designing the environment (willpower is limited, environment is constant)
- All-or-nothing thinking (missing one day does not erase progress)
