# Negotiation

Systematic framework for negotiating deals, partnerships, salaries, and contracts.

## Core Concepts

### BATNA (Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement)
- Your BATNA is your walkaway power — the best outcome if this deal fails
- **Before any negotiation**: Define your BATNA explicitly
- The stronger your BATNA, the more leverage you have
- Never reveal a weak BATNA; always work to strengthen it before negotiating
- Example: If negotiating salary, your BATNA is your next best job offer

### ZOPA (Zone of Possible Agreement)
- The range where both parties can agree (your minimum to their maximum)
- If no ZOPA exists, no deal is possible — walk away or change the terms
- Expand ZOPA by adding variables (not just price: terms, timeline, scope, equity)

### Reservation Price
- Your absolute bottom line — the point below which you walk away
- Set this before the negotiation, not during (prevents emotional concessions)
- Keep it private — revealing it gives away all your surplus

## Negotiation Strategies

### Principled Negotiation (Harvard Method)
1. **Separate people from the problem**: Attack the issue, not the person
2. **Focus on interests, not positions**: "Why do you want X?" reveals flexibility
3. **Generate options for mutual gain**: Brainstorm before committing
4. **Use objective criteria**: Market data, precedent, fair standards

### Anchoring
- The first number on the table heavily influences the outcome
- Make an ambitious first offer (within reason — extreme anchors backfire)
- If the other side anchors first, counter-anchor immediately (don't negotiate from their number)
- Anchor with data: "Market rate for this is $X" carries more weight than an arbitrary number

### Concession Strategy
- Never concede without getting something in return ("I can do X if you can do Y")
- Make concessions smaller over time (signals approaching your limit)
- Package concessions: offer 3 small things together rather than one at a time
- Track all concessions both sides make — maintain balance

## Negotiation Types

| Scenario | Key Leverage | Common Mistake |
|----------|-------------|----------------|
| Salary negotiation | Competing offers, skills scarcity | Accepting first offer without countering |
| Vendor contract | Alternative vendors, volume | Focusing only on price, not terms |
| Partnership deal | Unique capabilities, market access | Undervaluing your contribution |
| Client pricing | Value delivered, switching costs | Discounting too quickly |

## Tactics to Recognize

### Common Tactics Used Against You
- **Good cop / bad cop**: One tough, one friendly — recognize the dynamic
- **Deadline pressure**: "This offer expires Friday" — often artificial
- **Nibbling**: Asking for extras after agreement — resist or reopen negotiation
- **Silence**: Uncomfortable pause to pressure a concession — let the silence sit

## Preparation Checklist

- [ ] Defined your BATNA and worked to strengthen it
- [ ] Set your reservation price (absolute walkaway point)
- [ ] Researched the other party's interests, constraints, and alternatives
- [ ] Prepared your opening offer with supporting data
- [ ] Listed 5+ variables beyond price (terms, scope, timeline, exclusivity)
- [ ] Anticipated their objections and prepared responses
- [ ] Defined success: what does a good deal look like for both sides?
