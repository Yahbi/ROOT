# Presentation Skills

Design and deliver presentations that inform, persuade, and inspire action.

## Slide Design Principles

### Visual Hierarchy
- **One idea per slide**: If a slide needs two points, make two slides
- **6-word title rule**: Slide title should convey the key takeaway, not describe the content
- **Bad**: "Q3 Revenue Data" / **Good**: "Revenue grew 40% in Q3"
- Minimize text: max 20-30 words per slide (presenter fills in the rest verbally)

### Design Standards
| Element | Guideline |
|---------|-----------|
| Font size | Minimum 24pt body, 32pt+ titles |
| Colors | Max 3 colors from brand palette + gray for text |
| Charts | One chart per slide, labeled clearly |
| Images | Full-bleed or hero image, never stretched or pixelated |
| Animations | Subtle or none — animation is not content |

## Data Visualization

### Chart Selection
| Data Relationship | Chart Type |
|------------------|------------|
| Comparison (items) | Bar chart (horizontal for many items) |
| Comparison (time) | Line chart |
| Composition | Pie (< 5 slices) or stacked bar |
| Distribution | Histogram or box plot |
| Correlation | Scatter plot |
| Change over time | Line or area chart |

### Visualization Rules
- Always label axes and include units
- Start y-axis at zero for bar charts (avoid misleading scales)
- Highlight the data point that supports your message (color, annotation)
- Remove chart junk: gridlines, borders, legends (if only one series)
- Use consistent color coding across all slides in the deck

## Storytelling Structure

### Problem-Solution-Impact (PSI)
1. **Problem**: What pain exists? Make the audience feel it.
2. **Solution**: What are you proposing? How does it work?
3. **Impact**: What changes? Quantify the improvement.

### Situation-Complication-Resolution (SCR)
1. **Situation**: Context everyone agrees on
2. **Complication**: What changed or what's the challenge?
3. **Resolution**: Your recommendation and evidence

### Presentation Flow
- **Hook** (first 30 seconds): surprising fact, question, or bold claim
- **Body**: 3 key points max (audience remembers 3 things, not 10)
- **Close**: Clear call to action — what should the audience do next?

## Delivery Tips

### Preparation
- Rehearse at least 3 times out loud (not just in your head)
- Time each section — cut ruthlessly if over time
- Prepare for questions: anticipate top 5 objections and have data ready
- Have a backup plan if tech fails (PDF on USB, printed handout)

### During Presentation
- Speak to the audience, not the slides (know your content)
- Pause after key points (silence is powerful, not awkward)
- Use the "tell them" rule: tell them what you'll tell them, tell them, tell them what you told them
- Energy level should be one notch above the audience's energy
