# Stakeholder Management

Keep stakeholders informed, aligned, and supportive throughout projects and decisions.

## Stakeholder Mapping

### Power-Interest Grid
| | Low Interest | High Interest |
|---|-------------|---------------|
| **High Power** | Keep satisfied (executive sponsors) | Manage closely (key decision makers) |
| **Low Power** | Monitor (peripheral stakeholders) | Keep informed (team members, users) |

### Identification Process
1. List all people affected by or influencing the project
2. Classify each by power (can they block or approve?) and interest (do they care?)
3. Identify their key concerns, motivations, and communication preferences
4. Determine their current stance: supporter, neutral, or resistor

## RACI Matrix

### Roles
| Role | Meaning | Rule |
|------|---------|------|
| **R** - Responsible | Does the work | At least one per task |
| **A** - Accountable | Final decision maker | Exactly one per task |
| **C** - Consulted | Provides input before decision | Limit to 2-3 people |
| **I** - Informed | Notified after decision | As needed |

### Common Mistakes
- Multiple Accountable people per task (no one actually owns it)
- Too many Consulted people (decisions take forever)
- Responsible without authority to act (blocker)
- Not defining RACI at project start (confusion about roles throughout)

## Communication Plan

### Cadence by Stakeholder Type
| Stakeholder | Format | Frequency | Content |
|------------|--------|-----------|---------|
| Executive sponsor | 1-page summary | Weekly | Status, risks, decisions needed |
| Project team | Stand-up | Daily | Progress, blockers, plans |
| Broader team | Email update | Bi-weekly | Milestones, highlights |
| External stakeholders | Formal report | Monthly | Outcomes, metrics, roadmap |

### Communication Principles
- Lead with the conclusion, then supporting details
- Bad news travels up fast — never let executives be surprised
- Match format to audience: executives want summaries, engineers want details
- Repeat key messages 3-5 times through different channels

## Escalation Protocol

### When to Escalate
- Decision blocked for > 2 business days
- Risk materialized that exceeds project authority to resolve
- Scope change that impacts timeline or budget by > 10%
- Stakeholder conflict that cannot be resolved at current level

### Escalation Format
```
ISSUE: [One sentence description]
IMPACT: [What happens if unresolved — timeline, cost, quality]
OPTIONS: [2-3 options with pros/cons]
RECOMMENDATION: [Your preferred option with justification]
DECISION NEEDED BY: [Date]
```

## Alignment Techniques

### Getting Buy-In
1. Socialize ideas 1:1 before presenting to the group
2. Frame proposals in terms of stakeholder interests (not your interests)
3. Provide data and evidence, not just opinions
4. Offer choices (not "should we do X?" but "should we do X or Y?")
5. Document agreements and follow up in writing

### Managing Resistance
- Understand the root cause: fear of change, loss of control, lack of information
- Address concerns directly with empathy and evidence
- Find champions among resistors' peers (social proof)
- Start with a small pilot to reduce perceived risk
