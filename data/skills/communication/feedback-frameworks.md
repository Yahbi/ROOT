# Feedback Frameworks

Deliver feedback that is specific, actionable, and drives growth rather than defensiveness.

## SBI Model (Situation-Behavior-Impact)

### Structure
```
Situation: Describe when and where (specific, observable)
Behavior:  Describe what the person did (factual, not interpreted)
Impact:    Describe the effect on you, the team, or the outcome

Example (positive):
  S: "In yesterday's design review meeting"
  B: "you walked through the trade-offs of each approach with clear diagrams"
  I: "which helped the team reach consensus 30 minutes faster than usual."

Example (constructive):
  S: "During the sprint planning session this morning"
  B: "you interrupted three different people before they finished their estimates"
  I: "which caused two team members to stop contributing for the rest of the meeting."
```

### Why SBI Works
- **Specific**: Eliminates vague feedback ("you need to communicate better")
- **Behavioral**: Focuses on observable actions, not personality traits
- **Impact-focused**: Connects behavior to real outcomes (not just "I didn't like it")
- **Non-judgmental**: "You interrupted" is factual; "You're rude" is a judgment

### Adding Intent (SBI-I)
After delivering SBI, ask about intent:
- "Was that your intention?" or "What was going through your mind?"
- Separates impact from intent (most people do not intend negative impact)
- Opens dialogue rather than creating defensiveness

## Radical Candor

### The 2x2 Framework (Kim Scott)
```
                     CARE PERSONALLY
                          │
          ┌───────────────┼───────────────┐
          │               │               │
          │  Ruinous      │   Radical     │
          │  Empathy      │   Candor      │
          │  (nice but    │   (caring +   │
          │   unhelpful)  │    direct)    │
          │               │               │
  ────────┼───────────────┼───────────────┼──── CHALLENGE DIRECTLY
          │               │               │
          │  Manipulative │  Obnoxious    │
          │  Insincerity  │  Aggression   │
          │  (neither     │  (direct but  │
          │   caring nor  │   uncaring)   │
          │   challenging)│               │
          └───────────────┼───────────────┘
                          │
```

### Quadrant Behaviors
| Quadrant | Example | Result |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Radical Candor | "Your presentation missed the key metric the VP cares about. Let me help you revise it before tomorrow." | Person improves, feels supported |
| Ruinous Empathy | "Great job on the presentation!" (when it was not good) | Person does not improve, fails later at higher stakes |
| Obnoxious Aggression | "That presentation was terrible. How did you think that was ready?" | Person feels attacked, shuts down |
| Manipulative Insincerity | Compliments to face, criticizes to others behind their back | Trust destroyed, toxic culture |

### Practicing Radical Candor
- Give feedback within 48 hours of the event (context fades, feedback loses potency)
- Praise in public, criticize in private (one-on-one, not in team meetings)
- Ask for feedback on yourself first (model vulnerability, build trust)
- Start with: "Can I share some feedback?" (gives them a moment to prepare)

## 360 Reviews

### Design for Usefulness
| Element | Best Practice | Anti-Pattern |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Frequency | Biannual or quarterly | Only during annual review |
| Reviewers | 5-8 people (peers, reports, cross-functional) | Only manager |
| Questions | Behavioral and specific | Vague ("rate communication 1-5") |
| Anonymity | Anonymous for peers and reports | Attributed (causes political filtering) |
| Action | Shared themes + development plan | Filed and forgotten |

### Effective 360 Questions
- "What is [Name]'s most impactful strength? Give a specific example."
- "What one behavior change would make [Name] more effective?"
- "Describe a time when [Name] handled a difficult situation well."
- "Describe a time when [Name] could have handled a situation better."
- "If you could give [Name] one piece of advice, what would it be?"

### Processing 360 Feedback
1. Read all feedback at once (do not react to individual comments)
2. Identify themes: what appears in 3+ responses? (That is the real signal)
3. Separate emotional reaction from useful information (take 24 hours if needed)
4. Pick 1-2 themes to work on (not everything at once)
5. Share your development goals with your team (accountability + they notice the effort)

## Growth Conversations

### Career Growth Framework
```
Monthly 1-on-1 dedicated to growth (separate from status updates):

1. Reflect: "What did you learn or accomplish this month that you're proud of?"
2. Assess: "What skill gap is most holding you back right now?"
3. Plan: "What is one specific thing you can do in the next 30 days to develop that skill?"
4. Support: "What do you need from me to make that happen?"
5. Commit: Write down the development goal and review next month
```

### Giving Developmental Feedback
| Stage | Approach |
|-------|----------|
| Junior engineer | Frequent, specific, directive ("Do X because Y") |
| Mid-level engineer | Less frequent, more coaching ("What approach would you try?") |
| Senior engineer | Rare, strategic, peer-level ("Here's what I've observed, what do you think?") |

### The Feedback Sandwich Is Dead
- Positive-negative-positive is transparent and patronizing
- People learn to ignore the praise and dread what comes after "but..."
- Instead: be direct, be kind, and separate praise and criticism into different conversations
- Give praise when it is earned (not as a setup for criticism)

## Building a Feedback Culture

### Team Norms
1. Feedback is expected and welcomed (not something to fear)
2. Give feedback within 48 hours (timely)
3. Use SBI format (specific, behavioral, impact-focused)
4. Assume positive intent (ask about intent before judging)
5. Act on feedback received (close the loop: "You gave me feedback on X, here's what I changed")

### Feedback Rituals
| Ritual | Frequency | Format |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Sprint retrospective | Biweekly | Team-level: what went well, what to improve |
| 1-on-1 feedback | Weekly | Manager and report exchange feedback |
| Peer feedback | Monthly | Written feedback between collaborators |
| 360 review | Biannual | Comprehensive multi-source feedback |
| Skip-level 1-on-1 | Quarterly | Report's report talks to skip-level manager |
