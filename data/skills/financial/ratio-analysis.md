# Financial Ratio Analysis

Evaluate company health, profitability, and valuation using key financial ratios.

## Profitability Ratios

| Ratio | Formula | Good Signal |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| Gross Margin | (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue | >40% (software), >20% (manufacturing) |
| Operating Margin | Operating Income / Revenue | Expanding quarter-over-quarter |
| Net Margin | Net Income / Revenue | >15% indicates pricing power |
| ROE | Net Income / Shareholders' Equity | >15% consistently |
| ROIC | NOPAT / Invested Capital | >WACC (value creation) |

## Valuation Ratios

| Ratio | Formula | Context |
|-------|---------|---------|
| P/E | Price / EPS | Compare to sector median and 5yr history |
| PEG | P/E / EPS Growth Rate | <1.0 = undervalued relative to growth |
| P/S | Market Cap / Revenue | <2.0 for mature, <10.0 for high-growth |
| EV/EBITDA | Enterprise Value / EBITDA | <10 cheap, 10-15 fair, >20 expensive |
| P/FCF | Price / Free Cash Flow | <15 attractive for established companies |

## Leverage and Solvency

| Ratio | Formula | Warning Level |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| Debt/Equity | Total Debt / Equity | >2.0 is high leverage |
| Interest Coverage | EBIT / Interest Expense | <3.0 indicates distress risk |
| Net Debt/EBITDA | (Debt - Cash) / EBITDA | >3.0 = overleveraged |
| Current Ratio | Current Assets / Current Liabilities | <1.0 = liquidity risk |
| Quick Ratio | (Cash + Receivables) / Current Liabilities | <0.5 = immediate risk |

## Efficiency Ratios

| Ratio | Formula | Interpretation |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| Asset Turnover | Revenue / Total Assets | Higher = more efficient |
| Inventory Turnover | COGS / Average Inventory | Compare to sector peers |
| Days Sales Outstanding | (Receivables / Revenue) * 365 | Rising = collection issues |
| Free Cash Flow Conversion | FCF / Net Income | >80% = high quality earnings |

## How to Use

1. **Never evaluate a ratio in isolation** — compare to sector peers and historical trend
2. **Watch for divergences** — revenue up but FCF down = investigate
3. **Build a scorecard** — rate each ratio as green/yellow/red vs sector medians
4. **Track quarterly changes** — direction matters more than absolute level
5. **Flag manipulation** — if earnings grow but cash flow does not, dig deeper
