# Earnings Analysis

Systematic framework for trading around earnings announcements.

## Pre-Earnings Setup (1-2 weeks before)

1. **Gather consensus estimates** — EPS, revenue, guidance from analyst surveys
2. **Check whisper numbers** — options-implied move, Estimize crowd estimates
3. **Review prior quarters** — beat/miss history, management credibility score
4. **Map sector peers** — who already reported, what trends emerged
5. **Assess options pricing** — implied volatility vs historical realized move

## Key Metrics to Analyze on Release

- **EPS surprise**: actual vs consensus (>5% beat/miss is material)
- **Revenue surprise**: top-line growth matters more in growth stocks
- **Guidance revision**: forward guidance drives 60%+ of post-earnings move
- **Margin trends**: gross margin expansion/compression signals pricing power
- **Cash flow vs earnings**: divergence flags accounting quality issues
- **Segment breakdown**: identify which business units drive the surprise

## Trading the Reaction

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Beat + raise guidance | BUY on pullback from initial spike |
| Beat + lower guidance | SELL — guidance matters more than beat |
| Miss + raise guidance | HOLD — watch for reversal next session |
| Miss + lower guidance | SELL immediately, reassess at support |

## Post-Earnings Drift

- Stocks that beat tend to drift higher for 60 days (PEAD effect)
- Enter 1-3 days after report once volatility crush settles
- Size position at 50% of normal — earnings vol is unpredictable
- Set stop-loss at pre-earnings support level

## Risk Controls

- Never hold full position through earnings — sell half or hedge with options
- Max 3 earnings plays active simultaneously
- Close losing earnings trades within 2 sessions — do not hold hoping for reversal
