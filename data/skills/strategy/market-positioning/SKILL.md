# Market Positioning

## Purpose
Position products effectively: target customers, differentiation, value proposition, and messaging.

## Steps
1. Define the ideal customer profile (ICP) with demographics, pain points, budget, and buying behavior
2. Map the competitive landscape by identifying direct competitors, substitutes, and the do-nothing alternative
3. Identify the primary differentiation axis: price, speed, quality, data depth, ease of use, or specialization
4. Craft a value proposition in one sentence: for [target], [product] is the [category] that [key benefit] unlike [alternative]
5. Validate positioning against real customer language from reviews, forums, and sales conversations
6. Define pricing strategy relative to positioning: premium, competitive, or penetration
7. Create messaging hierarchy: headline, three supporting proof points, and one clear call to action

## Output Format
A positioning document with ICP definition, competitive map, value proposition statement, pricing rationale, and messaging framework.
