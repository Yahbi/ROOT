# Deep Work

Maximize cognitive output through structured focus periods and distraction elimination.

## Focus Block Design

### Time Blocking Rules
- Schedule 2-4 hours of uninterrupted deep work daily (morning is optimal for most people)
- Minimum block length: 90 minutes (less than this, you barely reach flow state)
- Maximum block length: 4 hours (diminishing returns beyond this)
- Buffer between blocks: 15-30 minutes (rest, walk, snack)

### Block Types
| Block Type | Duration | Purpose |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| Creator block | 2-4 hours | Writing, coding, designing, complex analysis |
| Decision block | 1-2 hours | Strategic thinking, planning, problem-solving |
| Learning block | 1-2 hours | Reading papers, studying new skills, experimentation |
| Admin block | 30-60 min | Email, messages, scheduling (batch these) |

### Starting a Focus Session
1. Define the single output you'll produce ("finish feature X", "write section Y")
2. Close all communication apps (Slack, email, social media)
3. Put phone face-down in another room
4. Set a visible timer (creates productive urgency)
5. Start with the hardest sub-task first (willpower is highest at the start)

## Environment Design

### Physical Environment
- Dedicated workspace for deep work (never use the same space for leisure)
- Noise management: noise-canceling headphones, white/brown noise, or silence
- Temperature: 68-72F / 20-22C (cognitive performance drops outside this range)
- Lighting: bright daylight-spectrum light reduces fatigue

### Digital Environment
- Block distracting websites during focus blocks (Freedom, Cold Turkey)
- Turn off all notifications (not just silence — fully off)
- Use full-screen mode for your primary work application
- Single browser tab for the task at hand (close everything else)

## Distraction Management

### Internal Distractions
- Keep a "capture list" nearby — write down intrusive thoughts to address later
- If a thought feels urgent, ask: "Will this matter in 24 hours?" (usually no)
- Hunger, thirst, bathroom — address biological needs before starting the block
- Anxiety about other tasks: review your task list before the block to know nothing is forgotten

### External Distractions
- Communicate boundaries: "I'm in focus mode until 12pm, message me after"
- Shared calendar with "Focus" blocks visible to team
- Designated check-in times: "I check messages at 9am, 12pm, and 4pm"
- Emergency channel: one phone number or DM that you'll answer (true emergencies only)

## Recovery and Sustainability

### Between Focus Blocks
- Walk for 10-15 minutes (movement restores attention)
- Look at something far away (20-20-20 rule: every 20 min, 20 seconds, 20 feet away)
- Avoid consuming content (Twitter, news) — let your brain idle

### Weekly Rhythm
- Schedule hardest deep work Monday-Wednesday (peak energy)
- Thursday-Friday: lighter deep work + collaborative work
- Protect at least 3 deep work blocks per week (non-negotiable)
- One full recovery day per week (no work, no productive guilt)

## Measuring Focus Quality

- Track hours of deep work per day (target: 4 hours)
- Track output per block (lines of code, words written, problems solved)
- Rate focus quality 1-5 after each block (identify patterns)
- Review weekly: which blocks produced the most value? Replicate conditions.
