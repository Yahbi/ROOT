# Focus Strategies

Protect and cultivate your ability to do sustained, concentrated work in a world designed to distract you.

## Pomodoro Technique and Variations

### Classic Pomodoro
- 25 minutes focused work → 5 minute break → repeat
- After 4 pomodoros: 15-30 minute long break
- During a pomodoro: no interruptions, no task switching
- If interrupted: the pomodoro is void, restart from zero

### Variations for Different Work Types
| Variation | Work Period | Break | Best For |
|-----------|-----------|-------|----------|
| Classic Pomodoro | 25 min | 5 min | Administrative tasks, email processing |
| 50/10 | 50 min | 10 min | Programming, writing, design |
| 90/20 | 90 min | 20 min | Deep creative work, complex architecture |
| Flowmodoro | Until focus breaks | Proportional | When you regularly achieve flow state |

### Flowmodoro Method
1. Start timer when you begin focused work
2. Work until your focus naturally breaks (do not force a time limit)
3. Break duration = work duration / 5 (worked 50 min → 10 min break)
4. Honors flow state instead of interrupting it at 25 minutes
5. Track session lengths to learn your natural focus rhythm

## Environment Design

### Physical Environment
| Factor | Optimal | Suboptimal |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Noise | White noise, ambient music without lyrics, silence | Conversations, unpredictable sounds |
| Lighting | Natural light, bias lighting behind monitor | Harsh fluorescent, dim lighting |
| Temperature | 20-22 C / 68-72 F | Too warm (drowsy) or too cold (distracted) |
| Desk | Clean, only current task materials visible | Cluttered with unrelated papers and devices |
| Seating | Ergonomic, varied (sit/stand/walk) | Static poor posture for hours |

### Digital Environment
```
Focus mode setup:
1. Close email client (not minimize — close)
2. Set Slack/Teams to Do Not Disturb
3. Close all browser tabs except the one you need
4. Phone: silent + face down (or in another room)
5. Block distracting sites: use Cold Turkey, Freedom, or /etc/hosts
6. Full-screen the app you're working in (removes visual distractions)
```

### Environmental Cues
- Designate a specific location or desk position for deep work
- Use a specific playlist that signals "focus mode" to your brain
- Wear noise-canceling headphones (even without music — signals "do not disturb")
- Consistent routine builds Pavlovian association: environment → focus

## Digital Minimalism

### Notification Audit
1. List every app that sends notifications
2. For each, ask: "Is this notification ever time-sensitive?"
3. Disable all that are not genuinely time-sensitive
4. Remaining notifications: batch into scheduled check-ins (3x daily max)

### Tool Reduction
| Category | Keep | Eliminate |
|----------|------|----------|
| Communication | 1 async (Slack) + 1 real-time (phone) | Multiple chat apps, social media DMs |
| Task management | 1 system (Todoist, Linear, or notebook) | Scattered sticky notes, mental lists, 3 different apps |
| Notes | 1 app (Obsidian, Notion, or paper) | Saving ideas in 5 different places |
| News/social | 1 scheduled time block (30 min/day) | Infinite scrolling throughout the day |

### Information Diet
- Unsubscribe from newsletters you do not read within 48 hours
- Remove social media from your phone (access only from a computer, intentionally)
- RSS feed with < 20 sources beats unlimited social media scroll
- Weekly information review: consume the best content from the week in one sitting

## Flow State

### Conditions for Flow (Csikszentmihalyi)
1. **Clear goals**: Know exactly what you are trying to accomplish in this session
2. **Immediate feedback**: See results of your actions quickly (tests pass, output appears)
3. **Challenge-skill balance**: Task is slightly beyond current ability (not boring, not overwhelming)
4. **No distractions**: Environment supports uninterrupted focus

### Flow Triggers for Knowledge Work
| Trigger | Implementation |
|---------|---------------|
| Challenge-skill ratio | Pick a task at ~4% above your comfort zone |
| Deep embodiment | Full-screen IDE, mechanical keyboard, tactile engagement |
| Rich environment | Multiple monitors with relevant context visible |
| Clear goals | Write the specific outcome before starting |
| Risk/stakes | Self-imposed deadline or public commitment |

### Protecting Flow
- Flow state takes 15-25 minutes to enter
- A single interruption resets the 15-25 minute clock
- Schedule 2-3 hour blocks minimum (90 min to enter flow + 60+ min in flow)
- Communicate boundaries: "I'm heads-down until 11:00 — ping me after that"

## Building Focus as a Skill

### Progressive Training
| Week | Target | Practice |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1-2 | 25 minutes unbroken focus | Classic Pomodoro, 4 per day |
| 3-4 | 50 minutes unbroken focus | 50/10 technique, 3 per day |
| 5-6 | 90 minutes unbroken focus | Single 90-minute deep work block |
| 7+ | 2+ hours in flow | Flowmodoro, track natural rhythm |

### Focus Recovery After Burnout
- Start with 15-minute focus blocks (rebuild gradually)
- Remove all optional inputs for 1 week (social media, news, non-essential meetings)
- Single-task aggressively: one tab, one app, one task at a time
- Physical activity before deep work (30-minute walk primes the brain for focus)
- Sleep 8+ hours (focus capacity is directly tied to sleep quality)
