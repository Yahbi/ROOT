# Competitive Analysis

Systematically analyze competitors to inform strategy, positioning, and product decisions.

## Market Mapping

### Competitor Identification
1. **Direct competitors**: Same product, same market (highest priority)
2. **Indirect competitors**: Different product, same problem (alternative solutions)
3. **Potential entrants**: Adjacent companies that could expand into your space
4. **Substitutes**: Non-software alternatives (spreadsheets, manual processes, agencies)

### Discovery Methods
- Google search: "[your product category]", "best [X] tools", "[competitor name] alternatives"
- G2, Capterra, TrustRadius: category listings and reviews
- Crunchbase: funding, team size, growth signals
- LinkedIn: competitor employee count and growth rate over time
- Customer interviews: "What else did you evaluate?"

## SWOT Analysis Framework

### Per Competitor
| | Helpful | Harmful |
|---|---------|---------|
| **Internal** | **Strengths**: What they do well (tech, brand, team, funding) | **Weaknesses**: Where they fall short (UX, pricing, support, gaps) |
| **External** | **Opportunities**: Market shifts they could exploit | **Threats**: Risks to their business (regulation, disruption, churn) |

### Your Position vs Each Competitor
- Map your strengths against their weaknesses (your attack points)
- Map their strengths against your weaknesses (your defense priorities)
- Identify gaps no one is addressing (blue ocean opportunities)

## Feature Comparison Matrix

### Building the Matrix
1. List all features across all competitors (include your product)
2. Categorize: must-have, differentiator, nice-to-have
3. Rate each: full support, partial, none
4. Weight by customer importance (from interviews and surveys)

### Example Structure
| Feature | You | Competitor A | Competitor B | Customer Importance |
|---------|-----|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| Core feature 1 | Full | Full | Partial | Critical |
| Integration X | Full | None | Full | High |
| Pricing model | Usage | Seat-based | Flat | Medium |

## Competitive Positioning

### Positioning Statement
"For [target customer] who [need], [product] is the [category] that [key differentiator] unlike [competitor] which [competitor limitation]."

### Positioning Strategies
- **Price leader**: Cheapest option (race to bottom — avoid unless you have scale advantages)
- **Feature leader**: Most comprehensive product (works if market values completeness)
- **Niche specialist**: Best for a specific segment (defensible, high margins)
- **UX leader**: Simplest and most pleasant experience (powerful in complex markets)
- **Integration leader**: Best connected to existing tools (ecosystem moat)

## Ongoing Monitoring

### Track Quarterly
- Competitor pricing changes (sign up for their emails, check pricing pages)
- New feature releases (follow their changelog, blog, social media)
- Hiring patterns (what roles = what they're building next)
- Funding rounds and acquisitions
- Customer reviews and sentiment (G2, Reddit, Twitter)
- Win/loss analysis: why deals were won or lost against each competitor
